The **Agent2Agent (A2A) Protocol**, introduced by Google, is designed to facilitate seamless communication and coordination among AI agents, regardless of their underlying technologies or vendors. It establishes a standardized method for agents to discover each other, exchange capabilities, delegate tasks, and synchronize actions without the need for custom-built integrations or shared memory. A2A employs JSON-RPC 2.0 over HTTP/HTTPS for communication, ensuring compatibility with existing web infrastructure. Security is managed through enterprise authentication patterns, including API keys, OAuth 2.0, and mutual TLS, providing robust access control mechanisms. ([developers.googleblog.com](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/a2a-a-new-era-of-agent-interoperability/?utm_source=openai))

In contrast, the **Agent Network Protocol (ANP)** focuses on enabling open-network agent discovery and secure collaboration using decentralized identifiers (DIDs) and JSON-LD graphs. ANP supports decentralized peer-to-peer interactions, allowing agents to autonomously discover and communicate with each other without centralized control. This approach emphasizes flexibility and scalability in agent interactions across diverse and distributed environments. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02279?utm_source=openai))

**Key Differences Between A2A and ANP:**

1. **Architecture and Discovery Mechanisms:**
   - *A2A:* Utilizes a client-remote agent model where agents expose their capabilities through "Agent Cards" accessible via HTTP. Discovery is typically manual or through static URL lookups. ([koyeb.com](https://www.koyeb.com/blog/a2a-and-mcp-start-of-the-ai-agent-protocol-wars?utm_source=openai))
   - *ANP:* Employs a decentralized peer-to-peer model, leveraging DIDs and JSON-LD for agent discovery and interaction, facilitating autonomous and dynamic discovery processes. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02279?utm_source=openai))

2. **Communication Patterns:**
   - *A2A:* Supports structured task delegation and coordination using JSON-RPC over HTTP, suitable for both synchronous and asynchronous interactions. ([developers.googleblog.com](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/a2a-a-new-era-of-agent-interoperability/?utm_source=openai))
   - *ANP:* Facilitates decentralized, peer-to-peer messaging with a focus on secure and flexible communication using JSON-LD schemas. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02279?utm_source=openai))

3. **Security Models:**
   - *A2A:* Implements enterprise-grade security through established authentication protocols like OAuth 2.0 and mutual TLS. ([developers.googleblog.com](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/a2a-a-new-era-of-agent-interoperability/?utm_source=openai))
   - *ANP:* Relies on decentralized identifiers and cryptographic methods to establish trust and secure communications in a decentralized manner. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02279?utm_source=openai))

**Adoption and Traction:**

As of early 2026, A2A has garnered significant support from over 50 technology partners, including major enterprises and service providers, indicating a strong industry inclination towards standardized agent interoperability. ([developers.googleblog.com](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/a2a-a-new-era-of-agent-interoperability/?utm_source=openai)) However, large-scale deployments are still emerging, and the protocol is considered to be in the early stages of enterprise adoption. ([xenoss.io](https://xenoss.io/blog/agent2agent-a2a-protocol-enterprise-guide?utm_source=openai))

Information regarding the adoption and traction of ANP is less prevalent, suggesting that it may not have achieved the same level of industry support or implementation as A2A. The decentralized nature of ANP presents unique challenges in standardization and widespread adoption.

**Unaddressed Gaps:**

Despite the advancements offered by both A2A and ANP, certain challenges in AI agent coordination remain:

1. **Standardization Across Protocols:**
   - The coexistence of multiple protocols like A2A, ANP, and others can lead to fragmentation, complicating interoperability across different systems and vendors. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02279?utm_source=openai))

2. **Dynamic and Context-Aware Coordination:**
   - Existing protocols primarily focus on communication and task delegation but may lack mechanisms for dynamic, context-aware coordination that adapts to changing environments and agent states.

3. **Scalability in Large Agent Networks:**
   - As the number of interacting agents increases, ensuring efficient and scalable coordination becomes challenging, particularly in decentralized settings.

Addressing these gaps requires ongoing research and development to create more robust, flexible, and scalable protocols that can handle the complexities of modern AI agent ecosystems.
